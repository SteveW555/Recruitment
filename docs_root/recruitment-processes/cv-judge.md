## üîë KEY PATTERNS FOR FUTURE CV CLASSIFICATION

### ‚úÖ **Strong Indicators of a GOOD, TAILORED CV:**

1. **Exact Keyword Matching**
   - Uses the EXACT tools/platforms mentioned in job description (Zendesk, Salesforce)
   - Uses the EXACT metrics mentioned (CSAT score, 90%+)
   - Uses the EXACT terminology (de-escalation, knowledge base, ticketing)

2. **Quantified Alignment**
   - Job says "90% CSAT" ‚Üí Candidate says "96% CSAT" (proves they exceed requirement)
   - Job says "high volume" ‚Üí Candidate says "60 tickets per day" (quantifies it)

3. **Responsibility Mirroring**
   - Job lists responsibility ‚Üí Candidate's experience mirrors it with similar phrasing
   - Example: Job says "create knowledge base articles" ‚Üí CV says "authored 50+ knowledge base articles"

4. **Industry-Specific Language**
   - Uses industry terminology: SaaS, Tier 1/Tier 2, B2B clients, escalation workflows
   - Shows understanding of the support ecosystem

5. **Tool Prioritization**
   - Places required tools prominently (Elena puts "Zendesk (Expert)" first)
   - Doesn't bury critical qualifications

6. **Rephrasing Job Requirements**
   - Takes job language and rephrases it naturally
   - Example: Job "explain technical concepts simply" ‚Üí CV "articulate solutions to non-technical users"

7. **Evidence of Research**
   - Mentions company type (SaaS), work style (remote-capable), or industry trends
   - Shows they understand the role context

---

### ‚ùå **Strong Indicators of a BAD, GENERIC CV:**

1. **Wrong Career Field Entirely**
   - Applying to customer support with DevOps/Design/unrelated background
   - No attempt to bridge the gap

2. **Zero Tool/System Overlap**
   - Job requires Zendesk ‚Üí CV lists Adobe Photoshop
   - Completely different software ecosystems

3. **Missing Critical Qualifications**
   - Job requires customer-facing experience ‚Üí CV says "No customer-facing duties"
   - Fundamental disqualifiers present

4. **Generic Career Objectives**
   - "Seeking opportunities in a dynamic environment" (could apply to any job)
   - No customization to THIS specific role

5. **One-Size-Fits-All Content**
   - Could be sent to 100 different jobs without modification
   - No evidence of reading THIS job description

6. **Wrong Skill Categories**
   - Job needs soft skills (empathy, communication) ‚Üí CV emphasizes hard technical skills (programming, infrastructure)
   - Mismatch in skill type

7. **Irrelevant Experience Emphasis**
   - Highlighting experience that has no connection to role requirements
   - No transferable skills attempted

**The strongest indicator of a suitable candidate is NOT just experience - it's EVIDENCE THEY READ AND TAILORED THEIR CV TO THIS SPECIFIC JOB.**
