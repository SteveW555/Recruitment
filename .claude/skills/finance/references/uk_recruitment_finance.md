# UK Recruitment Industry - Financial Metrics & Regulations

## Overview

This reference provides UK recruitment industry financial benchmarks, regulatory requirements, typical margins, and business metrics specific to 2024-2025. Use this as a guide for understanding ProActive People's financial performance relative to industry standards.

---

## UK Recruitment Industry Context (2024-2025)

### Market Size & Structure
- **UK Recruitment Market Value**: £38-40 billion annually
- **Number of Agencies**: ~30,000 recruitment agencies operating in UK
- **Average Agency Size**: 5-10 employees (ProActive People = 5)
- **Independent Agencies**: Make up 85% of the market

### Industry Bodies
- **REC (Recruitment & Employment Confederation)**: Primary industry body
- **APSCo**: Association of Professional Staffing Companies

## Revenue Models & Fee Structures

### Permanent Placement Fees

**Standard Fee Structure**:
- **Entry-Level Roles** (£20k-£30k salary): 15-18% of annual salary
- **Mid-Level Roles** (£30k-£50k salary): 17-20% of annual salary
- **Senior Roles** (£50k-£80k salary): 18-22% of annual salary
- **Executive Roles** (£80k+ salary): 20-25% of annual salary

**Industry Benchmarks (2024)**:
- Average fee percentage: 18-20% of first year salary
- Typical fee range: £3,500 - £25,000 per placement
- Payment terms: Net 30-60 days
- Rebate period: 8-12 weeks (full or partial refund if candidate leaves)

**ProActive People Positioning**:
- Fee range: 15-22% (competitive, mid-market)
- Average: 18% (industry standard)
- Target: 20-25 permanent placements per year

### Temporary Worker Margins

**Markup Structure**:
- **Standard Temp Workers**: 30-40% markup on pay rate
- **Skilled Temp Workers**: 35-45% markup
- **Specialist/Technical**: 40-50% markup
- **Executive Interim**: 50-60% markup

**Industry Benchmarks (2024)**:
- Average markup: 35-40%
- Typical gross margin: £150-£400 per worker per week
- Payment terms (from client): Net 7-14 days
- Payment terms (to worker): Weekly (immediate cash flow pressure)

**ProActive People Positioning**:
- Markup range: 35-42% (industry standard)
- Average: 38%
- Cash flow consideration: 7-14 day gap between paying workers and receiving client payment

### Retained Search & Consultancy

**Fee Structure**:
- **Stage 1 (Engagement)**: 30-40% of total fee (non-refundable)
- **Stage 2 (Shortlist)**: 30-40% of total fee
- **Stage 3 (Placement)**: 20-40% of total fee
- **Total Fee**: Typically 25-35% of annual salary

**Contact Centre Consultancy** (ProActive People specialty):
- Project-based pricing: £8,000 - £95,000
- Daily rate equivalent: £800-£1,500 per day
- Typical project: 10-60 days duration

## UK Regulatory Requirements (2024-2025)

### Employment Taxes & Contributions

**National Insurance (NI) - Major Changes April 2025**:

**Employer NI (2024)**:
- Rate: 13.8% on earnings above threshold
- Threshold: £9,100 per year (£758/month)

**Employer NI (April 2025 onwards - IMPORTANT CHANGE)**:
- Rate: **15.0%** (increase from 13.8%)
- Threshold: **£5,000 per year** (£417/month) - REDUCED from £9,100
- **Impact**: Significantly higher NI costs for all employers

**Employee NI**:
- Rate: 12% on earnings £12,570 - £50,270
- Rate: 2% on earnings above £50,270

**Income Tax (PAYE)**:
- Personal Allowance: £12,570 (2024-25)
- Basic Rate: 20% (£12,571 - £50,270)
- Higher Rate: 40% (£50,271 - £125,140)
- Additional Rate: 45% (above £125,140)

### National Living Wage & Minimum Wage

**April 2024 Rates**:
- National Living Wage (21+): £11.44 per hour
- 18-20 Year Old Rate: £8.60 per hour
- 16-17 Year Old Rate: £6.40 per hour
- Apprentice Rate: £6.40 per hour

**April 2025 Rates (CONFIRMED)**:
- National Living Wage (21+): **£12.21 per hour** (6.7% increase)
- 18-20 Year Old Rate: £10.00 per hour
- Other rates TBC

**Impact on Temp Workers**:
- Minimum hourly pay increases by 77p
- With 38% markup: Client charge rate increases by £1.07/hour
- Weekly impact: ~£43 per 40-hour worker

### Pension Auto-Enrollment

**Minimum Contributions (2024-25)**:
- Employer minimum: 3% of qualifying earnings
- Employee minimum: 5% of qualifying earnings
- Total minimum: 8%
- Qualifying earnings: £6,240 - £50,270 per year

**ProActive People**:
- Uses 3% employer contribution (minimum)
- Applied to all staff and eligible temp workers

### VAT (Value Added Tax)

**Standard Rate**: 20%
**VAT Registration Threshold**: £90,000 annual turnover (2024-25)

**Application to Recruitment**:
- Permanent placement fees: Subject to VAT (add 20%)
- Temp worker services: Subject to VAT (add 20%)
- Training services: Subject to VAT (add 20%)
- Most services: Standard-rated (20%)

**VAT Return Periods**:
- Quarterly (most common for SMEs)
- Due: 1 month and 7 days after quarter end
- Payment: Electronic (online banking or Direct Debit)

**Calculation**:
```
Output Tax (VAT charged to clients)
Minus: Input Tax (VAT paid on expenses)
= Net VAT due to HMRC
```

### Corporation Tax

**Rate**: 19% (for profits up to £50,000) - 2024-25
**Rates for larger profits**:
- 19% on profits up to £50,000
- Marginal rate: 26.5% on profits £50,001 - £250,000
- 25% on profits above £250,000

**Payment**:
- Due: 9 months and 1 day after accounting period end
- Quarterly installments for very large companies (£1.5m+ profits)

**ProActive People**:
- Likely in 19-25% bracket depending on annual profit
- Estimated annual profit: £225,000-£405,000
- Estimated tax: £42,750-£101,250

### GDPR & Data Protection

**Key Requirements**:
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) - recommended for recruitment
- ICO Registration: £40-£60 per year
- Lawful basis for processing candidate data
- Consent management for marketing
- Data retention policies (typically 6 months - 3 years)
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")

**Recruitment-Specific**:
- Candidate CVs: Lawful basis = legitimate interest or consent
- Marketing: Requires explicit opt-in consent
- Data sharing: DPA clauses in client/candidate agreements
- Retention: Delete data when no longer needed (typically after 6-12 months if not placed)

### Conduct of Employment Agencies Regulations 2003

**Key Requirements**:
- Prohibition on charging work-seekers (candidates)
- Requirement to verify work-seeker identity and right to work
- Provision of key terms in writing
- Restriction on withholding pay
- Advertising standards (truthful job descriptions)

### Professional Indemnity Insurance

**Requirement**: Not legally required but industry standard and often required by clients

**Typical Coverage**:
- £1 million minimum (small agencies)
- £5 million recommended (medium agencies)
- £10 million+ (large agencies or Executive Search)

**Annual Premium**: £2,000-£10,000 depending on coverage and claims history

### Right to Work Checks

**Requirement**: Employers (including agencies) must verify right to work in UK

**Process**:
- Check original documents (passport, visa, biometric residence permit)
- Take copies and record check date
- Repeat checks for time-limited permissions
- Penalties: Up to £20,000 per illegal worker

**ProActive People**: Compliance Officer (Emma Jane) manages all right-to-work checks

## Cash Flow Considerations

### Typical Payment Cycles

**Revenue Collection**:
- **Permanent Placements**: Net 30-60 days (average 45 days)
- **Temp Worker Invoices**: Net 7-14 days (average 10 days)
- **Consultancy Projects**: Net 45-60 days (large projects)
- **Training Services**: Net 30 days

**Cost Payment Cycles**:
- **Temp Worker Payroll**: Weekly (immediate)
- **Staff Salaries**: Monthly (28th of month)
- **Office Rent**: Monthly (1st of month)
- **Technology**: Monthly (subscription model)
- **HMRC (PAYE/NI)**: Monthly (by 22nd of following month)
- **VAT**: Quarterly
- **Corporation Tax**: Annually (9 months after year-end)

### Cash Flow Pressure Points

**Temp Desk Cash Flow Gap**:
- Pay temp workers: Week 1 (£1,000)
- Invoice client: Week 1 (£1,380 with 38% markup)
- Client pays: Week 2-3 (7-14 day terms)
- **Gap**: 1-2 weeks between paying workers and receiving payment
- **Solution**: Invoice factoring or working capital facility

**Permanent Placement Cash Flow**:
- Place candidate: Day 0
- Invoice client: Day 1
- Client pays: Day 30-60
- **Gap**: 1-2 months before fee received
- Rebate risk: Candidate may leave in first 8-12 weeks (partial/full refund)

**Seasonal Variations**:
- Q4 (Oct-Dec): Higher temp volumes (Christmas retail/events) = cash flow pressure
- Q1 (Jan-Mar): Training programs peak = good cash flow
- August: Recruitment slowdown = lower cash flow

## Profit Margins by Service Line

### Permanent Placements
- **Gross Margin**: 90-95% (low direct costs - mainly consultant time)
- **Net Margin**: 35-45% (after all overheads)
- **Break-Even**: ~8-10 placements per consultant per year

### Temporary Workers
- **Gross Margin**: 35-42% (markup on pay rate)
- **Net Margin**: 12-20% (after employer NI, pension, admin costs)
- **Critical Mass**: Need 15-25 active workers per desk to be profitable

### Training Services
- **Gross Margin**: 70-85% (trainer's time, materials minimal)
- **Net Margin**: 40-55% (higher margin than recruitment)
- **Scale**: Profitable at 10-15 programs per year

### Contact Centre Consultancy
- **Gross Margin**: 75-90% (consultant time, low direct costs)
- **Net Margin**: 50-65% (highest margin service)
- **Scale**: 2-3 major projects per year = £120k-£200k revenue

## Industry Trends & Challenges (2024-2025)

### Economic Factors
- **Inflation**: Still elevated at 3-4% (down from 2023 peak)
- **Interest Rates**: 4.75-5.25% (Bank of England base rate)
- **Labour Market**: Tight in some sectors, cooling in others
- **Unemployment**: ~4.2% (relatively low, favours recruitment)

### Regulatory Changes
- **April 2025 NI Changes**: Major cost increase for all employers
  - Employer NI: 13.8% → 15%
  - NI threshold: £9,100 → £5,000
  - **Impact**: £2,000-£3,000 extra per employee per year

- **National Living Wage**: £11.44 → £12.21 (April 2025)
  - **Impact**: Wage inflation for temp workers

### Technology Disruption
- **AI in Recruitment**: CV screening, candidate matching
- **Automation**: Job posting, candidate sourcing becoming automated
- **Video Interviewing**: Accelerated by COVID, now standard
- **Skills Gap**: Need for tech-savvy recruiters

### Market Challenges
- **Client Pressure on Fees**: Downward pressure on permanent placement fees
- **Payment Terms**: Clients extending payment terms (30 → 45 → 60 days)
- **Competition**: High number of agencies in market
- **Candidate Expectations**: Higher salaries, flexible working demands
